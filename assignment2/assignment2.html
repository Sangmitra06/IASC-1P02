Colors

Importance of colors

Colors show us the world. They give us the ability to define and distinguish between things around us. Studies show that some people find solace and peace by associating with a specific color(s). We all connect with different colors in different ways.

For instance, some people feel a sense of warmth when they look at the color red, while some danger.

Consciously or unconsciously, we form relationships with colors. Refer to relationship with colors for more information and insight into the relationship between colors and humans.

Uniqueness in colors

Red, yellow, and blue are colors we know and interact with on a daily basis. However, there exist innumerable colors that we've never even heard of before. Some of them are:

  • Glaucous: a soft shade of winter blue
  • Amaranth: a red-pink hue
  • Celadon: a pale green color

This just shows us how little we know about something we think we have complete knowledge of. The spectrum of colors is so vast that it is humanly impossible to name every color that exists!

The art of color mixing

Most of the unique colors that we speak about above are derived from the base colors (red, white, black, yellow, blue). Some other colors that we see everyday are also derived from these base colors, such as:

  1. Purple: Red+Blue
  2. Green: Blue+Yellow
  3. Orange: Red+Yellow

There are myriad ways in which we interact with colors apart from merely seeing them everyday. Sometimes we use them in our vocabulary as metaphors; certain companies use a specific color as their identity through symbolism, and often times we use color to describe our mood. We may not realize it, but most of our life revolves around color. It's a beautiful blessing that we get to interact with them!

Some paintings that instantly grabbed my attention

Castle and Sun-Paul Klee

Castle and Sun by Paul Klee was created in 1928. What I love about this painting is the use of a wide array of shapes alongside bright colors. The colors used are all bold, but they've been put together such that they complement each other really well.

Flint Castle-J.M.V Turner

Flint Castle by J.M.V Turner was created in 1838. Most of his paintings center around coastal areas across Europe and the rest of the world. What is enchanting about this painting is the way the sun, sea, and the coast are captured using a warm palette of colors, giving rise to a vivid sunset scene.
