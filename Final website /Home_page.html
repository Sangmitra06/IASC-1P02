What is deforestation?

Deforestation can be defined as the large-scale removal of trees from forests (or other lands) for the facilitation of human activities. It is a serious environmental concern since it can result in the loss of biodiversity, damage to natural habitats, disturbances in the water cycle, and soil erosion. Deforestation is also a contributor to climate change and global warming.

Causes of deforestation

The primary anthropogenic activities (human activities) that contribute to deforestation include:

  1. Agriculture: Small-scale and large-scale farming
  2. Logging: Cutting of trees for use as raw materials
  3. Mining and urban expansion: Clearing of forest area for the construction of infrastructure

Secondary causes include:

  1. The construction of homes and other buildings
  2. Agriculture (to meet the increased demand for food)
  3. The construction of roads, dams, and other infrastructure
  4. Overpopulation

Relatively rare causes:

  1. Destruction of forests due to hurricanes, floods, and other natural calamities
  2. Invasion of the forest ecosystem by parasites that destroy trees
  3. Forest fires that are sparked by lightning and other natural phenomena

Effects of deforestation

  1. Increased CO₂ levels
  2. Disturbance in the water cycle
  3. Soil erosion
  4. Effects on biodiversity
  5. Effects on economy
  6. Effects on human health

Reforestation and Afforestation

Reforestation mainly refers to the replanting of plants on those lands which have been destroyed for the benefits of mankind. Reforestation is not similar to afforestation. Afforestation mainly refers to the planting of trees, whereas, reforestation refers to the replanting of trees. The goals of both afforestation and reforestation are the same.
